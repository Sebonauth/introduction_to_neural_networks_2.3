The Neural Decision Process

Imagine you're at a party, trying to decide whether to join a conversation. You might weigh several factors: How many people you know in the group, how interesting the topic sounds, how tired you are. A neuron does something surprisingly similar when deciding whether to fire!

Let's see this process in action. When signals arrive at a neuron's dendrites, they're like votes either for or against firing. Some signals say 'fire!' (excitatory), while others say 'stay quiet' (inhibitory). The neuron adds up all these votes, and if they exceed a certain threshold – bang! The neuron fires, sending an electrical pulse racing down its axon.
