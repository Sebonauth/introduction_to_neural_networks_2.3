Anatomy of a Biological Neuron

A typical neuron consists of three main parts: the dendrites, the cell body (or soma), and the axon.

Continue

The dendrites are branching extensions that receive incoming signals from other neurons.

Continue

These signals are then transmitted to the cell body, where they are integrated.

Continue

If the combined signal is strong enough, the neuron generates an electrical impulse called an action potential.

Build Your Vocab

Action Potential

An action potential is a rapid rise and fall in electrical membrane potential that transmits signals along the neuron's axon.

Continue

The axon is a long, slender projection that carries the action potential away from the cell body toward other neurons.

Continue

At the end of the axon are the synaptic terminals, where the neuron communicates with other neurons via chemical messengers called neurotransmitters.

Continue

This process allows neurons to communicate and form complex networks that underlie all brain functions.

Why It Matters

Understanding the structure and function of biological neurons provides insights into how our brains process information and how we can model these processes in artificial systems.
